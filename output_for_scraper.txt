       <header class="entry-header">
        <h2>
         Informatics Ph.D. student to present at ACM-DEV 2015
        </h2>
        <h3 class="news_meta">
         November 24, 2015
        </h3>
       </header>
       <!-- .entry-header -->
       <div class="entry-content">
        <p>
         <img src="http://www.ics.uci.edu/bin/img/photos/noteworthy/noteworthy-AnkitaRaturi-2015.jpg" style="float: right; margin: 0 0 1em 1em; height: 90px; "/>
         Informatics Ph.D. student
         <strong>
          Ankita Raturi
         </strong>
         received an ACM Women in Computing (ACM-W)
         <a href="http://women.acm.org/scholarship">
          scholarship
         </a>
         to attend the
         <a href="http://acmdev.org/">
          ACM Symposium on Computing for Development
         </a>
         (ACM DEV), held at the Queen Mary University of London in December. ACM-W provides scholarships to enable women in computer science to attend research conferences around the world.
         <br/>
         <span id="more-3226">
         </span>
         <br/>
         At ACM DEV, Raturi will present on a paper she co-authored with current and former UC Irvine faculty Bill Tomlinson, Bonnie Nardi, Donald J. Patterson, Debra Richardson, Jean-Daniel Saphores and Dan Stokols. The paper, “
         <a href="http://www.djp3.net/?p=192">
          Toward Alternative Decentralized Infrastructures
         </a>
         ,” looks at how we can build interfaces between infrastructures to improve robustness, reliability and resilience. “Enabling communities to transition to a more resilient configuration of infrastructures is crucial for establishing a distributed portfolio of processes and systems by which human needs may be met,” Raturi says.
        </p>
        <p>
         This will be Raturi’s first time at the conference, “an ideal venue for this work to be presented,” Raturi says. The conference is a platform for “original and innovative work on the applications, technologies, architectures and protocols for computing in developing regions,” according to the ACM DEV website.
        </p>
        <p>
         “Having the opportunity to present my work, engage with the community and learn from leading researchers in my field is a major part of my professional growth,” Raturi says. “Discussing our work with experts who have been working on computing for development will be incredibly valuable.”
        </p>
       </div>
